NimbleDroid Product Update, March 2017
 

Welcome to our latest Product Update where we describe some of our recent feature releases. Let’s get started!

 
Network traffic introspection
 
Performance and functional problems can often be attributed to network-related code. We have added the ability to review network traffic requests, including the request URL and the timeline of the request. You can find this information for each Scenario in the left hand menu under Network Activity.
 
 
Easier collaboration for teams
 
Enterprise customers can now more easily share results amongst team members. Uploads by all team members can be made viewable without the need for explicit sharing. This capability can be made available to just admins or admins and developers, as desired. To find out more about enabling this for your team, email us at support@nimbledroid.com.
 
 
Full-screen graphs
 
Trending graphs now support full-screen viewing, you can find the icon in the top right of the graph section
 
 
Fail a build via Gradle
 
In addition to getting notified by Alerts, you can now actually fail a CI build that is using the Gradle plug-in. This allows you to set conditions such as a Scenario getting slower, a new memory leak, or an app crash to fail your build as a result.
 
 
Alerts for Scenarios getting faster
 
Since Alerts were introduced we have supported triggering when a Scenario gets slower, but we heard from some customers that they also wanted to know if there is a sudden speed-up for a Scenario. Speeds-ups can happen after deliberate changes to increase performance, but they can also be a symptom of unintended changes, like unintentionally removing a step in a workflow.
 
 
Custom fields for auto-login
 
Most apps rely on two fields, username and password, to authenticate users. Since it’s so common we built our auto-login code to support these two from the beginning. As NimbleDroid’s customer base has grown, we’ve heard from those apps that need something more than two fields. To that end we now support adding custom arbitrary fields when logging in.
 
 
 
That’s it for now, as always email us at contact@nimbledroid.com if you have any questions.
 
 

Josh Diehl, Director of Product
